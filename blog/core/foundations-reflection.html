Foundations reflections

What three new things you have learnt about yourself and your ego due to the core learning?

I’ve realised that sometimes my ego really wants to be the best - which can manifest in a number of ways, especially overthinking my expressions, or looking for evidence that I’m the best. This awareness is huge! It helps to be able to see when those tendencies are rearing their heads, so that you can see through those thought patterns which aren’t serving you.

I learnt that I don’t like to show weakness whether it’s a mistake that I’ve made or not communicating with the appropriate people when things are too much for me and not okay - Understanding this has been helpful because when I look back at situations where I could have shown weakness but didn’t, it’s clear that showing that weakness may have actually led to better outcomes, which hopefully makes it easier in future situations to let my weaknesses be seen.

I have been able to explore so many things that I’m proud of about myself and where I am today - Seeing all of the strengths, values, and parts of my identity that I have accumulated over the years is empowering. It’s easy to feel like an imposter sometimes, and being able to look at yourself and see so many good qualities is pretty fantastic!

What are the roles of values, empathy, and self-awareness in learning and programming?

Values influence the way you are in the world. It’s easy to say what your values are, while your actions might not align. By inspecting your values and writing about them it helps them become a clear part of your identity, and once they are, your boundaries can be clearer to yourself and others, and you can more easily live by your value set.

We need to have empathy because programming is a team-endeavour. If you can’t empathize with your team or they can’t empathize with you, it makes things quite challenging when making decisions, or whenever the slightest friction comes up. Empathy helps us to connect to each other and understand things from each other's shoes.

Self-awareness is important because when you are aware of your emotions, actions, values, limitations & strengths, then you can better adapt to situations. If you know you have a tendency to overthink things, then you can actually assess that you might be doing that in the moment and act accordingly to not waste time. Or if working in teams is new for you, it helps normalise the associated uncomfortable feelings. Or knowing that problem solving is a strength of yours so you can step up. In short, self-awareness helps make your strengths stronger and your limitations less limiting, so you can become the best version of you.

What has surprised you the most about the core learning?

Though I consider myself an emotionally intelligent person, this learning has helped integrate those values more deeply within me. There’s always more to learn, and even if you do have an understanding or belief, you can always go deeper. When you do go deeper, you find more pearls of wisdom to integrate into your life.

I found writing blog posts really helpful for integrating everything too. It’s easy to read or watch something, say yes I agree with that, then move on, but the act of writing about it ensures that you understand it at a deeper level, while making more connections between new learnings and existing information you already have.

What were the most challenging aspects of the core learning?

Trying to polish all my thoughts and feelings. Being comfortable that I’ve spent enough time working on them, that I’ve expressed myself clearly and concisely, that I haven’t rambled on too much that it’s boring, but I’ve left enough story in my posts that who I am comes across clearly.

I think I struggle with the concept of expressing myself in a readable blog post, when really no amount of text is going to accurately portray who anyone really is, or cover the whole depth of a topic. But I am getting more and more comfortable with this form of expression and allowing space for the reader to fill parts in. You’re smarter than I sometimes give you credit for.

Why do you think we, a programming school, are spending so much time focusing on core learning in a web development Bootcamp course?

Because in most situations, there’s no use being an amazing programmer if you can’t work with other people.

Core skills are critical for being able to work with yourself & others. I’ve been learning to code online for 3 years, so it’s the core part of foundations that I’ve found most valuable.

Even pre-foundations I was excited about pre planning how my weeks will look which just makes it so much easier to turn up and do what I said I would.

Creating a strong framework, timeboxing tasks, analysing problem solving skills, and knowing when to ask for help, getting clear on values, learning plans, goals, learning about emotional intelligence, neuroplasticity & growth mindset has been very powerful.

I already believed in or had a basic understanding of many of these concepts, but integrating them with my being and learning journey feels incredibly valuable.

Does the time you spent studying core learning here feel like a waste of time? Should you have just used that time to practise programming instead? Justify your answer.

No it doesn’t feel like a waste of time. I think the first place I was really exposed to similar levels of introspection was on a yoga teacher training in 2016, I loved it. What I learned on that yoga course was that introspection and knowing yourself is a great source of power. It enables you to be more true to yourself, and when you're more true to yourself you can live more honestly with other people.

For me, the core work on this course has just been building on top of that. I’m grateful to have already been exposed to core work so that it didn’t feel too daunting, and I’m stoked that Dev Academy has made core such an integral part of its identity and curriculum. It’s also nice to know that the other students are also doing the core work too which should make working with each other much smoother, creating an environment where creativity can flourish.
