Emotional Intelligence

What is Emotional Intelligence?

Emotional intelligence is the understanding and management of emotions. As much as the world may not feel emotional, most of our actions are affected by our emotions, and in some cases trying to elicit other emotions.

Therefore it’s helpful to have a deep understanding of emotions.

This understanding can help to navigate your own emotions by noticing when they’re hijacking you to do things that you don’t want to do or might regret. It can help you experience emotions fully without unconsciously reacting. Being aware of how things affect you helps you design and optimize your life.

Also, so that you can understand & navigate other people’s emotions by noticing what emotions other people are experiencing, empathizing with them, & understanding the causes of their emotions you can then navigate the situation more easily, connect with the other person more effectively & help each other.

These are the 4 main levels of emotional intelligence:

  • Perceiving emotions - Before you can understand & manage emotions, it’s helpful to first perceive emotions accurately, using non-verbal signals like body language and facial expressions.
  • Reasoning with emotions - To be able to use emotions to influence what we prioritize & pay attention to. Things that garner our attention cause emotional responses.
  • Understanding emotions - Being able to interpret the cause of a person's emotions. If somebody is responding to you with sad or negative emotions, the observer must find out what the underlying cause of that emotion is, is it a direct response to your actions, or is it related to other things going on in their life i.e. an argument they had with someone else, a personal loss.
  • Managing emotions - The height of emotional intelligence is the ability to manage emotions. This is where you can regulate your emotions and respond appropriately, as well as responding appropriately to another person's emotions.

How is EQ different to IQ?

EQ is more about understanding & navigating human emotions, while IQ is more focussed on general intelligence which includes problem solving & working memory.

IQ tends to be associated with these abilities:

  • Visual and spatial processing
  • World knowledge
  • Reasoning
  • Working & short-term memory

While EQ centres more on:

  • Identifying emotions
  • Perceiving how others feel
  • Controlling your own emotions
  • Relating to others
  • Using emotions for communication

After years of IQ getting all of the attention. It is now widely recognized that EQ is just as important, or even more important.

One of the main reasons that humans have been so successful on earth, is their ability to collaborate. That’s how the biggest projects are able to be pulled off. Therefore it makes sense that being able to work and communicate with others is a very important skill, what use is it to have all of the smarts, but not being able to collaborate with other people to do big things. Furthermore, even just getting by in family life, and society is much more manageable when you can relate to others.

IQ obviously still has its place, as mental skills are important. For getting things done and making good decisions. But one should hope to constantly improve in both areas for great success.

Why is EQ important?

EQ is important because emotional intelligence is all about being human (which we are!). Emotional intelligence helps us to connect with others & communicate effectively. It’s part of knowing oneself more intimately, to know what sets you off, what drives you, and where you have strengths and limitations.

Other skills associated with emotional intelligence include:

  • Listening to others fully
  • Expressing oneself accurately
  • Noticing other people's emotions
  • Empathy and concern for others
  • Reacting appropriately
  • The ability to let go of mistakes
  • Self confidence
  • Self acceptance
  • Managing your own emotions
  • Accepting and embracing change

There are so many more examples & all of these are rather helpful skills for working with other people, wouldn’t you say?

Knowing yourself well, and how to connect with others can make you much more productive, and are skills that aid in living a happier, more fulfilling life.
